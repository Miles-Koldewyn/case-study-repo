Tasha's Furry Friends

Non-Profit Redesign

Why Redesign Tasha's Furry Friends?

Before we conducted interviews, we felt the site was outdated, confusing and had way too many words. We wanted to solve this by finding out if users understood the cause and how TFF was providing solutions. Focus on a rebrand that pulls users into the cat's stories with a clean, crisp, easy to grasp page that will be built according to user feedback.

What we were working with

What the site looked like before the redesign

Interviews

During the ideation phase of the project, we conducted user interviews to further understand the problems with the current website and what we could do differently to enhance the users experience.

  • Can users quickly and easily understand what the cause of the non-profit is?
  • Is the current “Sponsor a Cat” page engaging, useful and direct?
  • What would be more helpful to have on the home page?
  • Do users feel connected to the cause?

Logo Design

Original Logo

Font choices and design make it hard to read. The colors are competing with each other the illustration is busy and too detailed.

Redesigned Logo

I simplified the design first to make the image more readable. I changed to the font to a sans serif so that it is more legible at a smaller size. I also kept it one color so that it can easily swap for light and dark backgrounds.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

We started with these Lo-Fi Wireframes to build out a basic idea of what we wanted the site to look like. We built these based of competitor analysis and user interviews. By doing this we optimized the flow and experience for the users coming to this site.

  • We used Figma to develop the Lo-Fi wireframes
  • We tried to keep the layout more open and clean so to combat the strange font choices and overwhelming feeling you got from the original site
  • After a few iterations we got to this basic design that worked well with our user tests

A/B Testing

Coming down the end of the project we coducted an A/B test to see what users thought about the new site compared to the old one. We were very happy with the results seeing that everyone preferred our compared to the original. UX/UI have profound impacts on the success of a business!

What I Learned

I learned how important it is for a site to clearly convey their purpose to the audience. Our site was so confusing at first but after the redesign it's so much more user-friendly.
